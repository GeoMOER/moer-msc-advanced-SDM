This unit provides a foundational understanding of how Species Distribution Modeling (SDM) has evolved and the diverse array of algorithms used to map species distributions.
<!--more-->


## Learning objectives

At the end of this unit you should be able to

* Differentiate between correlative and mechanistic modeling approaches.
* Identify the major families of SDM algorithms.
* Know the origins of the field.
* Recognize why certain technical defaults and evaluation metrics exist today based on historical breakthroughs.
* Explain the strengths and limitations of specific algorithms like MaxEnt, Random Forests, and GLMs.
